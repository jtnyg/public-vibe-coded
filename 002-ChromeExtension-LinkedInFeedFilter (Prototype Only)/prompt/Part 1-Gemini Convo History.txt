### 1. Validating the "MECE" Framework

By definition, MECE means **Mutually Exclusive** (no overlaps) and **Collectively Exhaustive** (everything has a home).

Currently, our categories are collectively exhaustive (thanks to the "General/Other" bucket), but they are **not mutually exclusive**.

* *Example:* "My company is hiring an AI Engineer!" -> Is this *Hiring* or *AI*?
* *Example:* A Sponsored post about a new AI tool. -> Is this *Sponsored* or *AI*?

**The Solution: A Waterfall Hierarchy**
To force mutual exclusivity, we must process the rules in a strict, top-down hierarchy. Once a post hits a trigger, it is categorized and exits the algorithm. Here is the proposed waterfall order:

1. **Sponsored:** (Highest priority. An ad is an ad, regardless of content).
2. **Job Seeking / Layoffs:** (High emotional weight; users usually want to specifically filter or support these).
3. **Hiring Roles:** (Actionable recruitment posts).
4. **AI Experience:** (General thought leadership/chatter about AI).
5. **General / Other:** (Catch-all).
